# Gear and Tech

All gear in ZaroVerse is **co-built** — alien precision combined with human instinct. No character receives magic equipment. They build it together.

## Design Principles

1. **Co-built:** Every piece is ARC-7's alien tech + human creativity
2. **Limited:** Every piece has costs, drain, and blind spots
3. **Character-expressive:** Each piece reflects its user's personality
4. **Franchise-ready:** Each piece can become a toy, game mechanic, cosplay item, or visual signature

## Kai — Before the Glove

Before any alien technology, Kai was already a builder working at the ceiling of what one pair of hands can do.

His workshop is the family garage — half-converted, the car still parks there at night, so every project has to be modular enough to pack up daily. That constraint shaped him. He builds fast, he builds with tolerance for being moved, and he never makes anything that can't survive being disassembled and reassembled in the dark.

Magnetism was always his native language. Pocket magnets, bench magnets, glove magnets — always in reach. His first real project was an electromagnetic crane built from salvage parts. He's still iterating on it. It's still in the garage.

The MagDust Glove didn't give Kai a new skill. It removed the physical limit on one he already had.

## Kai's MagDust Glove

A big, thick, heavy glove on Kai's dominant hand. Bulky by necessity — it houses the electromagnetic pulse generator that controls MagDust. Reinforced wrist cuff holds the power unit. Co-built with ARC-7's cyan containment tech.

It looks like a tool, not jewelry. The contrast with Mina's elegant bracelet is deliberate: her tech is fashion, his tech is industrial.

Kai gestures and MagDust in the environment responds — forming walls, shields, platforms, pipes, and structural braces.

**Limits:** Requires MagDust supply in the environment. Larger constructs drain faster. Must maintain focus. Can overheat with sustained heavy use.

**Visual:** Big, thick, rugged. Hand-built, not factory. Faint teal circuitry lines across the surface. Pulses when active — brighter = more power.

## Mina — Before the Fireflies

Before any alien technology, Mina was already a solo operator pushing what one person with one drone can do.

Her drone started as a commercial-grade hobbyist quadcopter. She has been rebuilding it ever since — custom firmware she wrote from scratch, a sensor package added piece by piece. The controller is wrapped in athletic tape, has a heat sink epoxied to the side casing, and two extra switches wired in below the standard sticks. Her thumbprints have worn shiny patches into the plastic where her hands live.

Her workflow: both hands on the controller, eyes split between the live camera feed and her laptop showing sensor readouts. Fully manual. One drone, total focus. When she's operating, the world ceases to exist.

The Fireflies system didn't give her a new way of thinking. It gave her a hundred eyes instead of one.

## Mina's Fireflies System

A three-component personal tech system:

**Bracelet + Finger Rig** — sleek bracelet that looks like jewelry, not tech. 5 thin wires run from the bracelet to finger sensors on each fingertip — the wires look like thin jewelry chain material, elegant and delicate. Hand gestures and finger movements control the swarm — Mina conducts the Fireflies like a musician. The bracelet is the power source and control hub with haptic feedback.

**Sensor Array** — small autonomous sensor drones that deploy in a perimeter pattern. They scan for Ink signatures, energy anomalies, and movement. These glowing points of light in the forest are the "fireflies."

**AR Glass** — single-eye augmented reality lens. Overlays threat data, energy readings, and dome status onto Mina's vision. Shows what normal eyes cannot see.

**Vision modes:** Mina can operate the swarm in three distinct perception states.

- **External Collective View** — she sees the swarm as a flock from outside. Best for formation, sweep coverage, and area patrol.
- **Individual Drone POV** — she drops her viewpoint into a single drone and sees what it sees. Best for close inspection and tracking through cover. While in this mode, she is unaware of the physical world around her.
- **Combined Spatial Scan** — all drones act as one distributed eye, rendering a real-time 3D model of the environment. The most powerful mode and the most mentally demanding. Requires the AR glass for full rendering. Causes fatigue and temporary disorientation on disconnect.

**Limits:** Drones have limited battery. AR glass can overload with data. Cannot detect dormant/hibernating Ink. Range weakens at distance.

**Visual:** Bracelet is slim with teal glow lines. Sensor drones are tiny floating lights. AR glass is sleek single-lens — not bulky goggles.

## The MemeWars Arena

ARC-7 creates a simulation arena for combat training — a controlled virtual environment projected within ZaroLand. His version is clinical: grid-based, teal wireframe, pure utility.

Kai and Mina transform it. They add color, music, energy, obstacles, scoring, and tactical feedback. The sterile sim becomes something alive and fun.

This is the **MemeWars arena** — where the team trains, experiments, and pushes each other to improve.

ARC-7 does not understand the name. He tolerates it.

## How the Gear Works Together

The gear is designed so that the team is greater than the sum of its parts:

- **Mina scans** → finds the threat and calls the position
- **Kai builds** → shapes barriers, structures, or conduits on her call
- **Zaro powers** → provides the energy that drives the defense
- **ARC-7 contains** → holds the line with precision containment

When all four work together, they can achieve things none could do alone — including amplifying the Verdant Seal into a focused, penetrating beam that can break through defenses ordinary Green cannot.
