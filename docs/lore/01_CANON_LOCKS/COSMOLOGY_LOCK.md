# COSMOLOGY LOCK (CANON)

Created: 2026-05-09 (working direction — revisable until shipped)
Companion: see DECISIONS_LOCKED_2026-05-09.md (`_To-Do/`) for the session that produced this lock.

This file establishes the architectural cosmology of the ZaroVerse — the rules that govern how the universe works at scales beyond Earth's CMY arc. It exists to protect the saga from accidentally closing the cosmos in any Book 1–3 decision.

This is *internal* canon. Most of it is OFF-PAGE in Book 1. Surfacing happens gradually across Books 2–7+ as the audience earns access.

---

## 1. CORE PRINCIPLE — UNIVERSAL PHYSICS, LOCAL STORIES

**The CMYK Prime System is a cosmic constant. The story you are reading is one of its instances.**

The frequencies are physics — Yellow expands and warms; Cyan contains and orders; Magenta transforms and disrupts; Black consumes. These rules apply *everywhere* in the universe.

The stories that emerge from CMYK interactions are *entirely local*. Different galaxies produce different Primes who make different choices and reach different outcomes. Some galaxies' triads united and won. Some lost. Some are still being written.

**The frequencies rhyme. The lives do not.**

This is the key distinction from typical multiverses. The ZaroVerse is not built on variants — there is no "alternate Zaro." There are *other Yellow Primes* in other galaxies who have nothing in common with Zaro except that they both condensed from the Yellow frequency under a star. Different forms. Different personalities. Different moral journeys.

**This is closer to Dune than Marvel.** Same physics, divergent fates.

---

## 2. THE FRACTAL COSMOS

The universe is fractal — *every star is a potential Yellow.* Every galaxy is its own CMY trial. Every realm has its own guardians.

Earth's story is one node among uncountable others. Earth is *where the reader entered the saga* — not where the saga centers.

| Element | Earth's region | The cosmos |
|---|---|---|
| Yellow Primes | Zaro is one Yellow. | Many other Yellow Primes exist across the cosmos. Some won. Some fell. Some are still emerging. |
| Cyan Primes | The 7-seat ARC Board (see §4). | Other realms have their own Cyan equivalents. Different rules. Different governance. |
| Magenta Primes | Aurora (Mina-coined nickname) is one Magenta. | Many other Magenta Primes exist. Different forms, different temperaments, different agendas. |
| Black Holes | Earth's Black Hole is *their* Black Hole — the one hunting their sun. | Uncountable other Black Holes across the cosmos. They are NOT a unified species. They are individual predators that emerged independently. They might compete. They might have killed each other in cosmic history readers will eventually learn. |

**The dark is a condition of the universe, not a person.** That's the long-game gift: the saga does not have to end with one final villain.

## 3. THE FIRST CYCLE (BOOKS 1–3)

The trilogy currently being written is **Cycle 1** — the story of Earth's CMY trial against its Black Hole.

| Cycle | Books | Arc |
|---|---|---|
| **Cycle 1** | Books 1–3 | Earth's CMY trial. Zaro / ARC-7 / Aurora form the team. White is achieved at climax of Book 3. The Black Hole is contained. |
| **Cycle 2** | Books 4–6 | The Consequences Cycle. Modified ARC lineage emerges. ZaroLand expands. Earth becomes contested as other cosmic forces notice it. |
| **Cycle 3** | Books 7–9 | The Recognition Cycle. Zaro and ARC-7 meet other galaxies' equivalents. Cross-Galaxy Recognition (see §6). The cosmos opens up. |
| **Cycle 4+** | Books 10+ | The Open Frontier. Multi-galactic stories. Side novellas. Licensed expansions. Parallel cycles told by other writers operating under the same physics. |

Cycles do not have to be written in order. A Book 17 could be set 4 billion years before Earth's events, in a galaxy that lost its trial. The cosmos is non-linear.

## 4. THE 7-SEAT ARC BOARD (CYAN GOVERNANCE)

The Cyan Prime governance is the only one detailed in Book 1 canon. Yellow and Magenta governance is parallel-but-different — TBD (see §5).

- **7 Board seats.** ARC Prime 1 through ARC Prime 7.
- **7 Rules.** Each Prime holds one corresponding Rule. Each Prime is the guardian and enforcer of their assigned rule. (Rule 7 = "Do not cross worlds." Rules 1–6 are mythology slots, TBD across Books 2–3.)
- **7 Cyan sub-bands.** Cyan spans 490–520 nm. Divided into 7 sub-bands of ~4.3 nm each. Each Prime sits at the center of their band. Drifting outside one's band is a *physical* sign of compromise — visible on any sensor.
- **ARC Prime 7** occupies the warmest band (~514–518 nm — the cyan closest to green/yellow, the band most prone to drift). He held the rule his band was structurally most likely to break. *He broke his own rule.*

**The Board's authority structure is, secretly, a personality spectrum.** Deepest cyan = most disciplined. Warmest cyan = most prone to drift.

**Cross-agent etiquette:** the Board's internal politics, individual Prime personalities, and the 6-vs-1 vote dynamic that dispatched the General are reserved for Book 2+ design.

## 5. PARALLEL GOVERNANCE (Y, M, K)

Each CMY color has its own internal governance. K does not.

- **YELLOW** band system. Yellow visible: ~570–590 nm. Total band count, division scheme, and rule structure are TBD. Different from Cyan's. Reserve for Book 7+ (when Zaro meets another Yellow).
- **MAGENTA** band system. Magenta visible: ~570–620 nm (the magenta range bridges red and violet). Aurora is one band's occupant. Other Magentas hold other bands. Different governance from Cyan's. Reserve for Book 2+ (when Aurora arrives in person and elements of her own realm surface).
- **BLACK has no band system.** K consumes the entire spectrum. No internal organization. No rule to break. No number to wear. The Black Holes are individual predators that emerged independently across the cosmos. *Black has no governance because Black has nothing to govern — only hunger.*

That last line is the Lore Vault hook. Reserve for Book 2+ codex fragment.

---

## 6. CROSS-GALAXY RECOGNITION IS SACRED

When a being from one galaxy's CMY meets a being from another galaxy's CMY, the moment must carry weight. They are NOT duplicates. They are NOT variants. They are first contact between two complete cosmic systems.

Reserve for arc-defining moments. Never make it routine.

The first time Zaro meets another Yellow is one of the most important moments in the saga. It is *not* repeatable cheaply.

Reserve for Book 7+.

---

## 7. WHY THIS IS LOCKED INTERNALLY (NOT BROADCAST)

The discipline is *whisper, don't broadcast.*

The cosmos is the careful reader's reward across re-reads. The casual reader gets a complete trilogy if Cycle 1 is all that ever ships.

The public website (per Q5b, 2026-05-09) reflects this with two-layer language:
- Drop "3-Book" framing in title meta and hero copy.
- Use "cosmic saga" — accurate without over-promising.
- One quiet line in the Lore Vault explainer: *"The CMYK system is a cosmic constant. The story you're reading is one of its instances."*
- Theorists catch the line. Casual readers don't. Both readings are complete.

**Never publish "20 books" or "endless saga" language in Book 1 marketing.** Earn it.

---

## 8. RULES FOR FUTURE WRITERS WORKING IN THE COSMOS

If other writers ever work in the ZaroVerse universe (licensing, side novellas, game expansions):

- They must obey CMYK physics. The frequencies are constants.
- They must NOT write "an alternate Zaro" or "Zaro from another timeline." That's variant slop. Their story is its OWN story under the same physics.
- They write THEIR galaxy's CMY trial — different Primes, different choices, different outcomes.
- Their stories are canon if they obey the physics.
- Earth is one node. Their galaxy is another. Neither is the center.

This is what gives the universe a 50-year sustainability that single-author franchises typically don't survive.

---

## 9. DO NOT VIOLATE (HARD RULES)

- Never close the cosmos in a Book 1–3 decision.
- Never frame Earth as the universe's center.
- Never allow "alternate" / "variant" framing for cross-galaxy CMY characters.
- Never give Black a band, a rule, a number, or governance.
- Never broadcast the saga's full scope publicly before earning it through Book 1.
