# VOCAB SIMPLE RULES (WRITING STYLE)


Changelog:
v2.1 — Clarified age target for book vs dialogue. Added prose register note.

ZaroVerse must feel global, clear, and cinematic.
Use simple words that translate well.

Rules:
1) Prefer short words over fancy words.
2) Avoid heavy science terms in dialogue.
3) Let visuals carry meaning.
4) Keep explanations minimal.
5) Keep UI words short and repeatable.

Examples:
Say: "Ink" not "mycelium."
Say: "Crack" not "dimensional rupture."
Say: "Air bubble" not "oxygen filtration field."
Say: "Power low" not "energy deficit."

ARC screens can be slightly more technical,
but still simple and readable.

Goal:
A 10-year-old can follow the story through dialogue and core ideas.
A 30-year-old finds the prose and systems cool.

BOOK PROSE NOTE (v2.1 — NEW):
This "10-year-old / 30-year-old" rule applies primarily to:
- Dialogue (spoken words between characters)
- UI text and on-screen labels
- Core story concepts and plot beats

Narrative prose in the novel may operate at a higher register:
- Primary reading level: 12+ (capable readers from age 10 can enter)
- Adult readers should feel respected, not talked down to
- Think early Philip Pullman or Ursula K. Le Guin's Earthsea
- Complex sentences are allowed when they serve rhythm and atmosphere
- Do not second-guess strong writing because it exceeds a 10-year-old's vocabulary
