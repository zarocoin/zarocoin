# NOT OVER SIGNAL RULES (CANON LOCK)


Changelog:
v1.1 — Added medium-specific expression rules. Clarified defiance origin.

"NOT OVER" is the Season 1 signature emotional mechanic.
It must stay consistent and earned.


### WHAT NOT OVER IS


NOT OVER is not a magic spell.
It is not a memory. It is not mystical.
It is pure defiance.

When Zaro is at his lowest:
his mind refuses to accept the end.
The phrase arrives because he chooses it.
No cosmic implant. No ancestral echo.
Just a being who will not quit.

It acts like a heart restart shock.


### MEDIUM-SPECIFIC EXPRESSION (UPDATED)


In ALL media:
Zaro carves a SYMBOL — not text. Not words in any language.
A universal visual mark from the CMYK system that means defiance.
"NOT OVER" is what Zaro THINKS. The symbol is what he CREATES.

In visual media (animation/series):
Zaro physically carves the symbol into the tree with his own light.
The audience sees the shape, not words.

In the novel:
Zaro carves the symbol. The narrative tells the reader
what it means through Zaro's thoughts and defiance.
The reader must feel that Zaro chose this.

The symbol works across every language, every medium,
every adaptation. Like Superman's S — no translation needed.


### WHAT IT DOES TO ZARO


Before NOT OVER:
- gray-yellow
- weak body
- dome flickers
- world begins to dim

When NOT OVER happens:
- instant snap back to full yellow
- sparks overflow like electricity
- dome slams back online
- Zaro surges into Overdrive

Cost:
Zaro gains a small gray scar.
This proves the power-up has consequences.


### WHAT IT DOES TO THE WORLD


The wave travels outward like a pulse.
It is strong enough to be sensed across realms.

Who senses it:
1) ARC-7 systems flood and alert — this triggers his overclock and Rule Seven break
2) The Black Hole feels it through the fungus network
3) (Future) Magenta Prime senses it from a distant galaxy

This is why NOT OVER is dangerous:
it reveals Zaro's existence to bigger forces.


### WHEN IT HAPPENS (LOCKED — BOOK 1)


NOT OVER occurs in Chapter 5 — The Breach / Storm chapter.
This is the chapter where:
- The fungus enters Zaro's home (Trojan breach)
- The fruits on the tree are SCORCHED — blackened, dimmed, lower ones nearly dead
  but still attached (LOCKED: fruits never scatter, they stay on the tree)
- The main tree outside is set on fire by the fungus
- Zaro's personal treasures are corrupted
- Zaro collapses emotionally
- Then he chooses: NOT OVER

1. Zaro is at his lowest. Gray-yellow. Dome failing. Tree burning.
2. He drags himself to the burning tree.
3. He CARVES A SYMBOL into the burned bark with his own light.
   Not text. Not words in any language. A SHAPE.
   A visual mark from the CMYK system that means defiance.
   (Specific symbol design TBD — must work across all languages/media.

### ZARO AFTER NOT OVER (LOCKED — PERMANENT CHARACTER CHANGE)


Zaro is permanently changed by the breach and NOT OVER.
The "new Zaro" from Chapter 6 onward is different from Chapters 1-4:

1. THE TREE SCAR: He sees it every day. It reminds him
   of what was taken and what he chose. It glows faintly.
   It is both wound and proof.

2. BROKEN SAFETY: Zaro can never fully trust his own space again.
   The crystal he loved was the weapon. The thing he chose
   to bring home destroyed his home. He now checks objects.
   He flinches at new things. He sleeps less soundly.
   The dome works. But Zaro's relationship with "safe" is broken.

3. MATURITY: The naive, wonder-filled Zaro of Chapters 1-4
   is gone. What replaces him is not hardened or cynical —
   but AWARE. He knows now that beauty can be a trap.
   He knows that the Ink is patient. He knows that love
   makes you vulnerable. And he chooses it anyway.
   That choice — knowing the cost and choosing anyway —
   is what makes post-breach Zaro a hero, not just a survivor.


### WHAT NOT OVER MEANS THEMATICALLY


NOT OVER is Zaro's core philosophy:
even when broken, he chooses to continue.

It becomes the franchise slogan.
It becomes the audience rally point.
It is CARVED INTO A LIVING TREE as a permanent scar.
It glows in the dark.

It must NEVER feel like lazy writing.
It must only happen when the audience truly believes:
he is finished.

Then it lands like a revolution.
