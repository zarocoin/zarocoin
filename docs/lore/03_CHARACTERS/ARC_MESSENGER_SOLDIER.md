# ARC MESSENGER SOLDIER

> **⚠️ SCOPE NOTE (2026-06-02).** This file defines the Soldier CLASS (a category of background ARC line units) and is the canonical **Ink-infection mechanics template** for ANY infected ARC being. The **on-page Chapter 11** infected-then-cured arrival is **the General** (an Observer of ARC-7's own kind — white-marble armor, teal DIAMOND insignia), NOT a Soldier. See `ARC_GENERAL.md`. The Soldier class remains valid for background / Book 2 ARC-Realm use; references below to the "first on-page test that earns the team" now point to the General.

The Soldier class is a category — basic line units that may appear as background ARC beings, including infected drones during ARC-Realm conflicts. The infection mechanics described below remain valid for ANY ARC being infected by Ink — Soldier, General, or Prime — with class-specific armor textures swapping in (Soldier: dark charcoal; General: white marble; Prime: dark charcoal).


3-LINE DEFINITION (Soldier class as a category)

Identity (who):
A Soldier-class ARC being is a tough, disciplined line unit built to carry out orders across the ARC world. Common, numerous, less independent than Generals or Primes.

Flaw (why relatable):
Soldiers are less intelligent than Prime-class ARC beings. They follow. They enforce. When they think for themselves, they are slower than a Prime would be — and that gap is what makes them vulnerable.

Power/Role (what they do in scenes):
They carry messages, hold positions, and execute ARC directives. Soldier-class units appear as background presence — line forces of the ARC Realm.


CLASS STATUS

Soldier class. Not Prime. Distinct from ARC-7 in intelligence, role, and body composition.

ARC beings are organized by class:
- PRIME CLASS (elite) — ARC-7's class. Rare, highly intelligent, refined.
- GENERAL CLASS (field command) — uncommon, larger build, marble armor.
- SOLDIER CLASS — this file. Tough, built heavier, less smart than Primes.

Soldiers are numerous. Primes are rare.


LOOK AND FEEL

Soldier-class ARC beings share the ARC-being signature but read differently:
- Adult humanoid proportions — roughly realistic adult scale (not compact like ARC-7's Prime proportions)
- Built for durability. Functional, combat-ready physique.
- Utilitarian plating — clean, purposeful, not ornate
- Less shoulder sculpting, less decorative detail, no prominent brow design
- Prominent hexagonal chest core, but smaller and more inset than a Prime's
- Same single vertical teal slit down center of brow
- Same ovoid teal eyes, whole-eye glow, no pupils
- Same dark charcoal shell + teal circuitry seams
- Same hex-motif plating language

Class signals through body composition and plate ornateness, not color. All ARC beings share the teal signature.


WHAT A SOLDIER IS NOT

- Not a villain. Not inherently dangerous. Pre-infection, they are ARC personnel doing their job.
- Not stupid. They follow protocol and execute well. They just don't have a Prime's capacity for judgment under anomaly.
- Not a Prime wielder. They do not create combination colors. Their teal is structural, not creative.


INFECTION CANON (THE INK)

When a Soldier is infected by the Ink, the visual signature corrupts subtly — not dramatically. The Ink hides inside its host the same way it hides inside the amber crystal Trojan: as MARBLING. Natural-looking veins inside a natural-looking feature. That's the Ink's rule — it camouflages, even when it's wearing a body.

THE RULE: TEAL STILL GLOWS. THE INK RUNS THROUGH IT.

Eyes:
- Whole-eye teal glow remains. Maybe slightly dimmer, slightly less saturated, but still readable as teal.
- A thick black rim hugs the inside edge of each eye's oval shape — kohl. The Ink frames the light from within, encircling it. The teal is visibly SURROUNDED by encroaching ink, not replaced by it.
- The host is clearly still there. Something is clearly wrong.

Seams and circuitry:
- Teal still glows along every circuit line.
- Fine BLACK MARBLING threads run through the teal — irregular, organic, like the veins inside the amber crystal Trojan. Same motif repeated inside the body.
- The Ink does not replace the Cyan Prime signature. It rides it.

Brow slit:
- Teal still lit. A thin black thread runs down the center of the glow, following the slit's geometry. Subtle.

Chest core (hexagonal):
- Teal hex still glows. Black marbling veins visible inside the geometry of the core — threading through the hex pattern. The power source is still outputting. The Ink is riding it.

Plating (charcoal shell):
- Hairline black marbling creeping across the armor plates. Low contrast — dark on dark. Reads almost like a natural design feature or stress pattern. Must be looked for to be seen.

The Ink ARMORS the host:
- The host becomes physically harder to touch, harder to penetrate, harder to reach. This is structural, not necessarily visible — it is felt when the team engages.

Sensory signature (canonical Ink cues):
- Edge distortion around the silhouette (peripheral shimmer goes wrong)
- Wet-static when the host moves
- Ambient sound dampens nearby
- Movement slightly out of sync — too smooth, or a half-beat late


INFECTION PROGRESSION

- EARLY: faint marbling in the seams only. Eyes clean.
- MID: marbling reaches the plating. A thin kohl rim forms inside the eyes. The Cyan Prime host is still in control.
- FULL: heavy kohl rim, marbling visible on seams / core / plating. The teal "self" is still there but now fully framed by ink.

THE HOST IS STILL INSIDE. THE BODY IS STILL RESCUABLE. THE INK IS NOT.


EXTRACTION VISUAL (Amplified Verdant Seal)

When the Amplified Verdant Seal hits, the marbling PULLS OUT through the eyes, seams, and plating — black threads drawn toward the Green beam like smoke toward wind, then burning to dust. The body is left behind: teal clean again, no kohl, no marbling. The host wakes.


WHY THE INFECTION WORKS THIS WAY

K (Black Hole) consumes; it does not produce Prime light. The Ink, as K's reach, cannot emit Prime color. The infection cannot flip the teal to another Prime frequency.

The marbling shows the Ink is ALIVE inside a still-living host. The Ink's Trojan behavior is to look natural — amber veins in a crystal, dark rim around an eye, stress lines across plating. If it's inside something, it disguises as a feature of that something.


WHY EXTRACTION REQUIRES THE AMPLIFIED VERDANT SEAL

Ink-infected hosts require the AMPLIFIED Verdant Seal:
- Green (C+Y fusion) alone cannot penetrate Ink armor on a host.
- The Amplified Verdant Seal — all four heroes + tree as focusing lens — generates a signal strong enough to pierce the armor and extract the Ink from the host body.
- The Ink is burned to dust. The host's body is saved.
- Ink is NEVER rescuable. Only the host is.


WHY THE SOLDIER CLASS MATTERS

The Soldier class is the canonical pattern for the Ink reaching inside the ARC Realm — a threat vector that was supposed to be impossible, proof that the Veil tunnel is not a one-way problem (the Ink can travel both ways). Soldier-class units carry this role as background / line forces, primarily in Book 2 ARC-Realm conflicts.

NOTE (Book 1 canon): the FIRST on-page infected ARC being is the General in Chapter 11, not a Soldier (see ARC_GENERAL.md). The General's cure is the moment that earns the team:
- ARC-7 alone cannot save an infected host (Cyan cannot pierce Ink armor on a host).
- Zaro alone cannot save an infected host (Yellow cannot pierce Ink armor on a host).
- Even the Controlled Verdant Seal cannot save an infected host (not strong enough).
- Only the Amplified Verdant Seal — all four + tree — can extract the Ink without killing the host.

This moment earns the team.


CORE TRAIT

Soldier class. Duty without a Prime's judgment. Tough shell. Vulnerable to the one threat that targets the system itself.
