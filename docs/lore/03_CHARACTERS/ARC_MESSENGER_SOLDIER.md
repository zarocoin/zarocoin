# ARC MESSENGER SOLDIER

## 3-LINE DEFINITION

Identity (who):
The Messenger Soldier is a Soldier-class ARC being — tough, disciplined, built
to carry out orders across the ARC world.

Flaw (why relatable):
Soldiers are less intelligent than Prime-class ARC beings. They follow. They
enforce. When they think for themselves, they are slower than a Prime would be —
and that gap is what makes them vulnerable.

Power/Role (what they do in scenes):
They carry messages, hold positions, and execute ARC directives. In Book 1,
one Messenger Soldier becomes the first known Ink-infected host inside the
ARC Realm — the Ch10 crisis that forces the Amplified Verdant Seal.

## CLASS STATUS (LOCKED)

Soldier class. Not Prime. Distinct from ARC-7 in intelligence, role, and
body composition.

ARC beings are organized by class. The two locked classes so far are:

- PRIME CLASS (elite) — ARC-7's class. Rare, highly intelligent, refined.
- SOLDIER CLASS — this file. Tough, built heavier, less smart than Primes.

Soldiers are numerous. Primes are rare.

## LOOK AND FEEL

Soldier-class ARC beings share the ARC-being signature but read differently:

- Adult humanoid proportions — roughly realistic adult scale (not compact like ARC-7's Prime proportions)
- Built for durability. Functional, combat-ready physique.
- Utilitarian plating — clean, purposeful, not ornate
- Less shoulder sculpting, less decorative detail, no prominent brow design
- Prominent hexagonal chest core, but smaller and more inset than a Prime's
- Same single vertical teal slit down center of brow
- Same ovoid teal eyes, whole-eye glow, no pupils
- Same dark charcoal shell + teal circuitry seams
- Same hex-motif plating language

Class signals through body composition and plate ornateness, not color.
All ARC beings share the teal signature.

## WHAT A MESSENGER SOLDIER IS NOT

- Not a villain. Not inherently dangerous. Pre-infection, they are ARC personnel doing their job.
- Not stupid. They follow protocol and execute well. They just don't have a Prime's capacity for judgment under anomaly.
- Not a Prime wielder. They do not create combination colors. Their teal is structural, not creative.

## INFECTION CANON (THE INK — LOCKED)

When a Messenger Soldier is infected by the Ink, the visual signature
corrupts subtly — not dramatically. The Ink hides inside its host the
same way it hides inside the amber crystal Trojan: as MARBLING. Natural-
looking veins inside a natural-looking feature. That's the Ink's rule —
it camouflages, even when it's wearing a body.

## THE RULE: TEAL STILL GLOWS. THE INK RUNS THROUGH IT.

### Eyes

- Whole-eye teal glow remains. Maybe slightly dimmer, slightly less saturated, but still readable as teal.
- A thick black rim hugs the inside edge of each eye's oval shape — kohl. The Ink frames the light from within, encircling it. The teal is visibly SURROUNDED by encroaching ink, not replaced by it.
- The host is clearly still there. Something is clearly wrong.

### Seams and circuitry

- Teal still glows along every circuit line.
- Fine BLACK MARBLING threads run through the teal — irregular, organic, like the veins inside the amber crystal Trojan. Same motif repeated inside the body.
- The Ink does not replace the Cyan Prime signature. It rides it.

### Brow slit

- Teal still lit. A thin black thread runs down the center of the glow, following the slit's geometry. Subtle.

### Chest core (hexagonal)

- Teal hex still glows. Black marbling veins visible inside the geometry of the core — threading through the hex pattern. The power source is still outputting. The Ink is riding it.

### Plating (charcoal shell)

- Hairline black marbling creeping across the armor plates. Low contrast — dark on dark. Reads almost like a natural design feature or stress pattern. Must be looked for to be seen.

### The Ink ARMORS the host

- The Messenger becomes physically harder to touch, harder to penetrate, harder to reach. This is structural, not necessarily visible — it is felt when the team engages him.

### Sensory signature (canonical Ink cues — unchanged)

- Edge distortion around the silhouette (peripheral shimmer goes wrong)
- Wet-static when he moves
- Ambient sound dampens near him
- Movement slightly out of sync — too smooth, or a half-beat late

### INFECTION PROGRESSION (if ever depicted in ramp)

- **EARLY:** faint marbling in the seams only. Eyes clean.
- **MID:** marbling reaches the plating. A thin kohl rim forms inside the eyes. The Cyan Prime host is still in control.
- **FULL (Ch10):** heavy kohl rim, marbling visible on seams / core / plating. The teal "self" is still there but now fully framed by ink.

## THE HOST IS STILL INSIDE. THE BODY IS STILL RESCUABLE. THE INK IS NOT.

### EXTRACTION VISUAL (Amplified Verdant Seal, Ch10)

When the Amplified Verdant Seal hits, the marbling PULLS OUT through
the eyes, seams, and plating — black threads drawn toward the Green
beam like smoke toward wind, then burning to dust. The body is left
behind: teal clean again, no kohl, no marbling. The host wakes.

### WHY THIS DESIGN (the logic)

- K (Black Hole) consumes; it does not produce Prime light. The Ink, as K's reach, cannot emit Prime color. The infection cannot flip the teal to another Prime frequency without breaking CMYK canon.
- Pure black replacement would read as a dead machine. The marbling shows the Ink is ALIVE inside a still-living host.
- The Ink's Trojan behavior is to look natural — amber veins in a crystal, dark rim around an eye, stress lines across plating. If it's inside something, it disguises as a feature of that something.
- The rating is PG / 10+. Subtle dread beats horror-film shock. The team has to REALIZE he's infected, not be told by a visual billboard.

This is why Ink-infected Messengers require the AMPLIFIED Verdant Seal:

- Green (C+Y fusion) alone cannot penetrate Ink armor on a host.
- The Amplified Verdant Seal — all four heroes + tree as focusing lens — generates a signal strong enough to pierce the armor and extract the Ink from the host body.
- The Ink is burned to dust. The Messenger's body is saved.
- Ink is NEVER rescuable. Only the host is.

## WHY THE MESSENGER SOLDIER MATTERS

The Messenger Soldier is the first canonical example of the Ink reaching
inside the ARC Realm. A ripple. A threat vector that was supposed to be
impossible. The crisis that proves the Veil tunnel is not a one-way
problem — the Ink can travel both ways.

He is also the first real test of the team as a team:

- ARC-7 alone cannot save him (Cyan cannot pierce Ink armor on a host).
- Zaro alone cannot save him (Yellow cannot pierce Ink armor on a host).
- Even Controlled Verdant Seal cannot save him (not strong enough).
- Only Amplified Verdant Seal — all four + tree — can extract the Ink without killing the host.

This moment earns the team.

## CORE TRAIT

Soldier class. Duty without a Prime's judgment.
Tough shell. Vulnerable to the one threat that targets the system itself.

## FORBIDDEN FOR SOLDIER-CLASS DESIGN

- Do not give them Prime-ornate plating or distinctive markings
- Do not give them a glow palette other than teal (Cyan Prime signature) before infection, or darkened/ink-black after infection
- Do not render them as visually similar to ARC-7 in proportion — Prime proportions are compact/youthful; Soldier proportions are adult/realistic
- Do not have them speak like a Prime — measured but less precise; more functional than philosophical; they are not Mode 3 speakers by default
- Do not make them villains pre-infection. They are the infected, not the enemy. The enemy is the Ink.
