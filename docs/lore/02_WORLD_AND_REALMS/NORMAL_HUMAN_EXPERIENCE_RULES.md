# NORMAL HUMAN EXPERIENCE RULES (CANON)

Version 1.0

These rules define what ordinary humans can perceive, feel,
and understand about ZaroVerse phenomena.

This prevents plot holes and keeps the world grounded.


### THE DOME — HUMAN PERCEPTION


Rule: Humans CAN faintly perceive the dome.

What they experience:
- A warm shimmer, like heat-haze rising off hot pavement
- A subtle feeling of warmth when crossing the boundary
- The shimmer is faint — easy to dismiss as "sun through trees"
- No visible wall, no color, no sound

What they CANNOT perceive:
- The dome's exact boundary (only instruments can map it)
- The dome's connection to Zaro's emotional state
- Any yellow or colored light from the dome itself

Implications:
- Mina and Kai felt "something warm" before they had tech proof
- A random hiker COULD walk through the dome and just think "nice warm spot"
- The shimmer is not enough to attract attention on its own
- Mina's drones are what confirmed the dome as an energy boundary


### SPARK DUST — HUMAN INTERACTION


Rule: Spark Dust is INERT to normal humans.

What they experience:
- It looks like fine glitter (golden, faint)
- It sits on surfaces like pollen or dust
- Touching it feels like nothing — just tiny particles
- No warmth, no static, no sensation

What they CANNOT perceive:
- Its connection to Zaro or the Seed Core
- Any energy signature (instruments only)
- Its slow fade over time (it dissipates in days)

Implications:
- A human could pick up Spark Dust and think it's mica or mineral flake
- Mina's instruments detect its unusual spectral signature
- Spark Dust is the breadcrumb that leads Mina to investigate
- It is NOT dangerous to humans in any way


### THE CONTAINER CRASH — WHY NO AUTHORITIES


Rule: The crash was noticed but explained away.

What happened:
- The Container entered atmosphere at night
- It was visible as a bright streak — like a meteor
- Impact occurred in rural isolated forest (The Greenbelt)
- Local news reported a "suspected meteor" sighting
- A brief investigation followed:
  - Forestry service checked the area within 48 hours
  - Found a scorched patch and some displaced earth
  - No metallic fragments (the Container dissolved/absorbed)
  - Classified as "small meteorite, no significant impact"
  - Report filed, no follow-up

Why it was dismissed:
- Small meteors hit Earth regularly — this isn't unusual
- The Greenbelt is remote, on defunct private land
- No property damage, no injuries, no public interest
- The Container left no conventional debris
- Spark Dust looks like natural mineral residue
- The dome was active but undetectable by the investigation team
  (humans perceive it only as a faint warm shimmer — easily dismissed
  as sun-warmed air in a summer forest)

What remains:
- A brief local news article (Mina finds this during her research)
- A forestry service report (Easter egg opportunity)
- A small online thread of "meteor watchers" who logged the trajectory
- None of this attracted serious scientific attention

Implications:
- Mina finds the news article — this is part of her investigation trail
- The "meteor report" is a Tier 1 Easter egg in the book
- No government conspiracy, no cover-up — just bureaucratic disinterest
- This is realistic: small meteor impacts are genuinely common and ignored


### THE INK — HUMAN PERCEPTION


Rule: Humans cannot directly perceive the Ink as a threat.

What they experience:
- A feeling of unease (like being watched)
- The air feels heavier, slightly damp
- A faint smell: stale, metallic, "wrong"
- Shadows seem darker than they should be
- Animals go quiet (humans notice the silence, not the cause)

What they CANNOT perceive:
- The Ink itself as a visible entity (unless it's in heavy concentration)
- Wet-static sound texture (this is sensed by Zaro and tech, not human ears)
- The Ink's intelligence or intent
- Fungal threads spreading on surfaces (too fine for naked eye until advanced)

At heavy concentration:
- Humans CAN see dark patches that look like mold or water damage
- They CAN smell rot/decay
- They still won't understand what they're seeing
- They'll assume "damp spot" or "mildew" — not cosmic threat

Implications:
- Mina's drones detect Ink presence before any human could
- A normal human in an Ink-affected area would feel "creeped out" but not endangered
- The Ink does not attack normal humans (they have no light worth consuming)
- Normal humans are invisible to the Ink — it hunts light, not flesh


### WET-STATIC SOUND — HUMAN PERCEPTION


Rule: Humans CANNOT hear wet-static.

- Wet-static is the signature sound of Ink proximity
- It exists at a frequency below/above human hearing range
- Zaro feels it instinctively (it sets his light on edge)
- ARC-7's instruments detect it precisely
- Mina's drones can detect it as an anomalous audio signature
- Normal human ears hear nothing — they just feel "uneasy"


### SUMMARY: WHAT NORMAL HUMANS EXPERIENCE


DOME: Warm shimmer, like heat-haze. Easy to dismiss.
SPARK DUST: Inert glitter. No sensation. Looks like mineral flake.
CRASH SITE: Scorched earth, growing back. Nothing obviously alien.
INK (light): Unease, heavier air, stale smell. Explainable.
INK (heavy): Visible mold/dark patches. Still explainable.
WET-STATIC: Nothing. Below perception threshold.
ZARO: If a human saw Zaro, they would see... a small glowing yellow creature.
        This would NOT be explainable. This is why Zaro stays hidden.
