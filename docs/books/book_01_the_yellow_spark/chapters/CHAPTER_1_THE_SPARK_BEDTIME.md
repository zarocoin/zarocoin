# CHAPTER 1 — THE SPARK

✦ ✦ ✦

Far away, in a place that was not quite space, someone was watching.

He saw a small gold light. Falling toward a small blue world.

He had seen many lights before.

This one was different.

He watched it fall.

✦ ✦ ✦

Down on the world, the forest was sleeping.

Oak and maple and birch. Moonlight in the leaves. A creek hidden under ferns. Crickets singing the kind of song that means everything is okay.

Then the sky opened.

A gold line came down. Through the clouds. Through the trees. Into the soft dark earth.

The forest hushed.

The crickets stopped.

A bright cloud rose from the new crater. Tiny golden dust, glowing only when the moon caught it just right.

Spark Dust.

For three breaths, the whole forest was full of stars.

Then the dust settled.

And the forest went very quiet.

At the center of the crater sat a small round stone. Smooth. Warm-colored. About the size of your fist.

It pulsed once. Gold. Cyan. A quick flash of pink. Alive.

Then the pulse stopped. The stone went cold.

But it was only sleeping. Sleeping things look different from gone things.

This one was sleeping.

✦ ✦ ✦

A yellow light rose above the crater.

It floated. It tried a shape. Then another. Then it chose.

Arms. Legs. A small round body. A small soft face. Pink cheeks. Eyes that shone like a candle behind glass.

Two little feet touched the ground.

The light was no longer just light.

It was a boy.

Zaro opened his eyes.

He took a slow breath. He had hands. Warm gold hands, glowing soft at the tips, like candlelight under skin.

"Okay," he whispered.

He looked up. Stars. So many stars.

Looking at them made his heart hurt. Like remembering something he had never seen.

He looked down. He saw the small dark stone.

He stepped close. He knelt. He picked it up.

It was cold.

"Hi," he said. "Hi, are you in there?"

Nothing.

He shook it gently.

Nothing.

"Sorry, little one," he whispered. "Sorry."

He held the stone close to his chest and walked into the trees.

✦ ✦ ✦

He found a small cabin behind the vines.

The door creaked when he opened it. A long, slow creak — like the cabin had been waiting a very long time for someone to come.

Inside was dust and moonlight. A broken chair. A cracked lamp. A window with one missing pane.

Zaro put the stone on the table.

"Wake up," he whispered. "Please wake up."

He put his hand on the stone. He pushed warmth into it, gently. Like holding your hand near a fire.

Nothing happened.

"Okay," Zaro whispered to himself. "Okay. One more try."

He held the stone in both hands. Like holding a tiny baby bird.

He waited.

His own light grew softer. He was giving warmth to the stone. The stone was taking it.

He did not stop.

He would not stop.

And then —

Warmth.

Not his warmth. The stone's.

A tiny heartbeat came back. Gold. Cyan. A flash of pink. Then soft, steady, amber.

The stone glowed in his hands like a nightlight.

"There you are," he whispered. His voice shook a little. "There you are."

The stone pulsed. Steady. Alive. Warm.

Zaro held it to his chest.

"I got you," he whispered. "I got you. Stay."

His breath went in. The stone pulsed. His breath went out. The stone pulsed.

Two little hearts beating in the dark.

He sat down on the floor and held the stone for a long time.

✦ ✦ ✦

The stone dimmed a little. The cabin was cold. The damp came up through the floor.

Zaro stood up.

He touched the cracked wall. He let warmth move from his hand into the wood. The wood listened. The crack closed.

He touched the floor. The floor warmed.

He touched the lamp. The lamp glowed soft and gold.

The cabin felt better now. Warmer. Quieter. Like it had been holding its breath for a long time, and now it could let go.

Zaro smiled a small smile. Without meaning to.

He was a little tired. A little dimmer than before. But the stone was warm. The lamp was warm. The walls were warm.

He found a small wooden box in a cupboard. He put the stone inside. The amber light filled the box.

"Stay safe," he whispered.

These were the most important words he had said yet.

He hid the box under a floorboard, in a small dry hollow. He pressed his palm to the wood. The wood promised to keep the stone safe.

✦ ✦ ✦

Outside, something arrived.

Not a sound. A feeling. Like the air had grown heavy.

The lamp flickered.

Zaro turned.

The forest had gone too quiet. He could see the creek through the missing pane, but he could not hear it.

A little moth flew in. Small wings. Soft and silent.

It crossed a shadow on the windowsill.

The moth stopped. Mid-air.

For a second, its wings went the wrong color. Pale and powdery.

Then the moth fell to the sill. Still alive. Just slower. Like the air had forgotten how to hold it.

Zaro saw.

He understood.

His hands rose before he chose them.

Warmth spread out from his chest. Through the cabin. Across the floor. Up the walls. Out past the window.

Then it locked with a small clean click.

A warm bubble. All the way around the cabin.

Zaro touched the air near the missing pane. It pushed back. Gently. Like a promise.

He was tired now. A little dimmer. But the bubble was holding.

"Good," he whispered.

He sat down before he fell.

The little moth was still on the sill, breathing slow.

Zaro cupped his hand around it and held it close to the lamp.

"You're okay," he whispered. "You're okay."

The moth trembled. Then settled. Warm. Safe.

✦ ✦ ✦

Outside, the dark mist came closer.

Zaro did not know what it was.

He only knew it belonged outside.

It moved the way smoke moves, but smoke needs air, and somehow this needed nothing.

The bubble hummed. Low and steady. Holding.

Inside the bubble, the stone pulsed under the floor.

Inside the bubble, the moth slept in Zaro's hand.

Inside the bubble, the lamp glowed amber.

Zaro sat with his back against the wall.

He was tired.

But the stone was warm. The lamp was warm. The moth was warm. The cabin was warm. And the bubble was warm.

The dark mist waited outside.

But the bubble held.

And Zaro was safe.

And the stone was safe.

And the moth was safe.

And that was enough for tonight.

✦ ✦ ✦

END OF CHAPTER 1
